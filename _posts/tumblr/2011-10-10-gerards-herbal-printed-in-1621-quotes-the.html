Gerard’s Herbal, printed in 1621, quotes the English planter John Goodyer on Jerusalem artichokes: “which way soever they be dressed and eaten, they stir and cause a filthy loathsome stinking wind within the body, thereby causing the belly to be pained and tormented, and are a meat more fit for swine than men.”
