From Ashley’s latest email:

Does Anheuser-Busch Make the Best “Craft” Beer?

Consumer Reports has an article out on the “Best Craft Beers” in which the editors tasted and rated 23 ales and lagers. Some of their top picks, like Stone IPA, we can’t really argue with.

But included in their list were some odd “craft” choices, such as Blue Moon Belgian White, made by MillerCoors, and Shock Top Wheat IPA, made by Anheuser-Busch InBev. The article acknowledges that “craft beer” can be a “somewhat squishy” term, but really, is it so squishy that two of the world’s largest industrial breweries fit into the category?

Let’s review. The Brewers Association defines a craft brewery as one that is small (less than six million barrels in annual production), independent (less than 25% owned or controlled by a corporate entity like AB InBev), and traditional (basically, they don’t water down their beers). We don’t really think of any of those things when we see this:

Not so small or traditional

Still, even though Consumer Reports obviously doesn’t “get it,” this kind of attempt to jump in front of the parade has to be considered progress. When Ashley’s was founded 30 years ago, not only were there no Stones around to be noticed by such a mainstream magazine, there is no way Consumer Reports would have given them the time of day even if they had been.
