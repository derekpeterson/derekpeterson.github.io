"The highlighted claim from 2002 was actually one of the few things Goldberg said back then about Iraq which was accurate: Saddam really did massively increase his efforts to obtain nuclear weapons after the Israeli air attack. Indeed, at the time of the 1981 strike, Iraq had no meaningful nuclear weapons program; it was the 1981 Israeli attack that spawned the intense efforts to develop one. That’s the effect of attacking a country: incentivizing them (and others) to obtain nuclear weapons in order to deter future attacks."
