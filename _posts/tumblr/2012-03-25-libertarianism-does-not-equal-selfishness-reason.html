A piece arguing that, while libertarianism considers economics an essential approach for understanding people, it subscribes to an alternative conception of economics. While the popular idea is that economics is about the pursuit of monetary ends, libertarianism contends that economics is about the pursuit of ends in general. People are behaving economically when they choose to call their friend rather than reading a book just as much as when they ride a bus rather than driving their car.
