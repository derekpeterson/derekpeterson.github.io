"Nearly 60 percent of Orleans Parish students now attend a public school that meets state standards, more than twice as many as did before Hurricane Katrina."

Given that Orleans Parish lost just under 100,000 residents (one-quarter of its population) because of Hurricane Katrina, I have to wonder whether the absolute change in the number of students attending such schools is as drastic as this number implies. It’s wonderful that 60% attend schools meeting the state standards, but some part of this jump has to come at the expense of some of the city’s poorest residents’ permanent moves to other locales. I wish the authors gave more context.
