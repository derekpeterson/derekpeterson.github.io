I bought a Bodum French press coffee-maker (1-liter Kenya model; highly recommended) from Amazon two weeks ago. That tells them that I like coffee. So, naturally they would tailor recommendations for me based on this recent purchase. That’s only logical. What did I just receive? One of their “you might also like to buy” emails that featured Bodum coffee-makers. Why would the algorithm suggest that that’s a relevant line of products to offer me? I have no idea. I understand that they don’t sell many coffee-related products outside of coffee-makers, which puts a natural limit on the number of connections they can make between my recent coffee-maker purchase and other products. But why suggest that I buy what I just bought? Why doesn’t Amazon’s recommendation algorithm have an anti-circularity feature? Why not just recommend that I buy a toaster to accompany my French press?
