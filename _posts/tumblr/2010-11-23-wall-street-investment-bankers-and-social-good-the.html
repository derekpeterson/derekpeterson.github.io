This article explains why we can (and should) debate whether Wall Street’s biggest activities actually produce any good. It seems clear that the banks don’t produce anything approaching what they themselves take. Rather than optimizing outcomes and making the markets efficient, they move money from one person to another, all the while stashing a pretty chunk in their own pockets.
