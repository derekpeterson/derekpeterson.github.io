Matt Steinglass on the American shift away from offering asylum to dissidents in Vietnam, the Soviet Union, or Cuba to closing our borders to helpful Iraqis and Afghanis. The difference is our crippling fear of terrorism. The result is that, as American forces leave, the people who cooperate with them lack the means to escape the punishment that awaits.
